## The Intake Problem That Costs Law Firms Millions

A prospective client searches "car accident lawyer" at 9:47 PM. They click on your ad, land on your website, look around for 30 seconds, and leave. No form submission. No phone call. That click cost you $75-150 in ad spend and returned nothing.

Now imagine a different scenario: the same visitor lands on your site and a chat window appears. The bot asks a simple question — "Were you recently in an accident? I can help you understand your options." The prospect engages. Within 90 seconds, the bot has collected the accident date, injury details, insurance information, and contact number. By 9:52 PM, a qualified lead is sitting in your CRM with a consultation booked for tomorrow morning.

That is the difference between a law firm that captures leads and one that watches them disappear.

## How the Bot Qualifies Without Practicing Law

The legal intake bot is not a lawyer and does not pretend to be one. It is a structured intake system that asks the right questions in the right order:

- **Case type identification** — The bot determines whether the inquiry involves personal injury, family law, criminal defense, estate planning, immigration, or another practice area you handle.
- **Jurisdictional screening** — Where did the incident occur? Where does the prospect reside? The bot filters out cases outside your geographic practice area immediately.
- **Statute of limitations check** — When did the event happen? The bot flags time-sensitive cases for urgent attorney review.
- **Severity and merit assessment** — For PI cases: What injuries were sustained? Was medical treatment sought? For criminal defense: What charges were filed? Is there an upcoming court date?
- **Conflict pre-screening** — The bot collects opposing party names and checks against your database before any attorney engagement.

Every qualifying question has a purpose: ensuring that the leads reaching your attorneys are worth their time.
