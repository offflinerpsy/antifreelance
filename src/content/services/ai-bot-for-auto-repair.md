## Why Auto Repair Shops Are Losing Customers to Silence

The average independent mechanic shop misses 30-40% of incoming calls during peak hours. Your technicians are under a lift, your service writer is handling a walk-in, and the phone rings into voicemail. That caller? They just booked with the shop down the street that answered on the first ring — except it wasn't a person. It was an AI bot.

Auto repair is a trust-driven business. Customers want fast answers, transparent pricing, and the confidence that their vehicle is in good hands. An AI chatbot built specifically for auto repair delivers all three — instantly.

## How the Bot Works in a Real Shop Environment

When a customer visits your website or sends a message on Facebook, the bot greets them and asks what they need:

- **Routine maintenance** — Oil change, tire rotation, brake inspection? The bot shows availability and books the slot immediately.
- **Diagnostic concerns** — Strange noise, warning light, performance issue? The bot walks them through a structured intake form and collects photos.
- **Estimate requests** — The bot pulls from your configured pricing to give ballpark ranges, setting expectations before the visit.
- **Status updates** — Customers check repair progress without calling, freeing your front desk from repetitive inquiries.

## Built for the Way Shops Actually Operate

Unlike generic chatbots, our auto repair bot understands the workflow of a service bay. It knows that a brake job takes longer than an oil change. It knows to ask about vehicle year, make, and model before quoting. It knows that Saturday mornings fill up fast and suggests alternatives when slots are tight.

The bot also handles the revenue-critical moments that happen outside business hours. A flat tire at 9 PM, a dead battery on a Sunday morning — these are the moments when customers need to know someone is listening. Your bot captures that intent and converts it into a booked appointment before your competitor even opens.
