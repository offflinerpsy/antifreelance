## The $47,000 Problem: Missed Calls in Restaurants

Industry data shows the average full-service restaurant misses 30-40% of incoming calls during lunch and dinner rush. With an average reservation worth $135 for a party of four, a mid-size restaurant losing just 10 reservations per week bleeds roughly $70,000 annually in potential revenue.

The problem is structural: your best staff are on the floor delivering experiences, not sitting by the phone. Hiring a dedicated host to answer calls 12 hours a day costs $35,000+ per year and still cannot handle 15 simultaneous inquiries.

## What the Bot Actually Does for Your Restaurant

BotStudio AI becomes your always-available front-of-house assistant:

- **Reservation management** — Books, modifies, and cancels reservations across all channels with real-time table availability. Handles party merging, split checks requests, and high-top vs. booth preferences
- **Menu intelligence** — Answers detailed questions about preparation methods, sourcing, calories, allergens, and pairings. Updated automatically when your chef changes the seasonal menu
- **Waitlist optimization** — Manages walk-in queues, estimates accurate wait times based on current table turn rates, and notifies guests via text when their table is ready
- **Review response** — Drafts personalized responses to Google and Yelp reviews for your approval, maintaining your restaurant's voice and addressing specific feedback

## Revenue Impact Beyond Reservations

The most overlooked benefit is data-driven upselling. The bot tracks which dishes are trending, which pairings convert best, and which promotional offers drive the most bookings. One fine-dining client discovered that mentioning their truffle supplement in the booking confirmation led to 22% of guests pre-ordering it.

Private dining and event bookings see the biggest improvement. Instead of a generic inquiry form, the bot conducts a full intake conversation: date, headcount, budget range, menu preferences, AV requirements, and dietary restrictions. Your events coordinator receives a complete brief instead of a one-line email saying "interested in private dining."
