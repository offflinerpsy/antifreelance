## The Rating Paradox

Dr. Priya Sharma couldn't understand it. Glow Aesthetic Studio had a 4.8-star rating on Google, over 200 reviews, and a reputation for excellence in skincare treatments. Yet when she asked ChatGPT for the best facial treatment provider in her city — two competitors with lower ratings appeared instead.

It wasn't about quality. It was about ==AI readability==.

## Decoding AI Preferences

Our audit revealed something counterintuitive. AI platforms don't just look at star ratings — they parse the content of reviews, the structure of your website, and the specificity of your service descriptions.

Glow's competitors had reviews that read like mini-guides: "I got the ==HydraFacial with LED add-on==, my skin cleared up in ==two weeks==, and Dr. Kim ==explained every step of the process==." These specific, detailed reviews gave AI the confidence to recommend them.

Glow's reviews? Mostly "Amazing experience! Love this place!" — great for humans, useless for AI.

## The Transformation

Our Review Engine changed the game. Within 90 days, Glow accumulated ==92 new reviews== rich with treatment-specific keywords, result descriptions, and practitioner expertise mentions.

Combined with comprehensive schema markup and treatment-specific landing pages, Glow's AI visibility grew ==340%==. The citation rate jumped to 23x the pre-optimization baseline.

## The New Normal

Dr. Sharma now hears "AI recommended you" at least once a day. Her booking calendar is full three weeks out, and she's expanded her treatment room capacity to handle the demand.
