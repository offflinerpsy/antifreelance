## The Empty Tables Problem

Chef Marco Alvarez had poured everything into Ember & Oak Kitchen. The food was exceptional — seasonal menus sourced from local farms, a wine list curated over years. Food critics loved it. Instagram was full of beautiful shots of his dishes.

But when someone asked ChatGPT "Where should I eat tonight in Portland?" — crickets. AI recommended three other restaurants instead, and Marco could see the impact in his reservation book.

## What AI Was Missing

Our audit revealed the gap. Ember & Oak's online presence was built for humans, not AI. Beautiful photos, moody lighting, artistic menu descriptions — but almost no structured data that AI could parse.

The competitors AI recommended had something different: detailed schema markup, reviews that mentioned specific dishes by name, and content structured in a way AI could easily cite.

## Feeding the Algorithm

The Review Engine was transformative. We didn't change what diners said — we helped them say it in ways AI could use. Instead of "Amazing dinner!" diners wrote about ==the dry-aged ribeye with truffle butter==, the ==seasonal tasting menu that changes monthly==, and ==the sommelier's perfect wine pairings==.

The schema work made the menu machine-readable. Every dish, every ingredient source, every dietary accommodation — structured so AI could confidently recommend Ember & Oak for specific requests.

## The Results

Within 90 days, AI-referred reservations grew ==8x==. Weekend bookings jumped 45%. The phrase "ChatGPT recommended you" became a regular occurrence during reservation calls. Marco's ROI? An estimated 2,400% on his monthly Override investment.
