## The Market Shift

Rachel Torres had been in real estate for 20 years. She'd survived market crashes, interest rate hikes, and the Zillow revolution. But this was different. Home buyers were showing up to open houses saying things like "Perplexity recommended another agent for this neighborhood."

AI was becoming the new referral network — and Pinnacle wasn't in it.

## The Visibility Gap

Our audit was eye-opening. We tested 25 different queries across 7 AI platforms. Not a single one mentioned Pinnacle Realty Group. Not for general real estate, not for specific neighborhoods, not for any of their 15 agents.

A boutique agency with ==3 agents== was being recommended instead — because their website had neighborhood-specific content that AI could easily parse and cite.

## Neighborhood by Neighborhood

The strategy was hyperlocal. We created detailed neighborhood guides that covered everything AI platforms look for: ==market trends, school ratings, commute times, lifestyle amenities, and price ranges==. Each guide was structured with schema markup that mapped to how AI categorizes real estate expertise.

The Review Engine added client stories tied to specific neighborhoods: "Rachel helped us find the perfect home in ==Westside Heights==, negotiated ==$40K below asking==, and closed in ==21 days==." These neighborhood-specific signals built AI confidence in Pinnacle's local expertise.

## The Results

By month 3, Pinnacle appeared in ==all 7 AI platforms== we tracked — the only real estate company in their market to achieve complete coverage. Leads grew 5x, with $2.1 million in closed deals directly attributed to AI referrals.

Rachel's agents now introduce themselves at open houses as "the agents AI recommends."
