## The $15,000 Problem

David Sterling was spending $15,000 per month on Google Ads to keep his law firm visible. It worked — but the cost per lead kept climbing. Then he noticed something: potential clients were starting conversations with "I asked ChatGPT for a lawyer and it didn't mention you."

That was the wake-up call.

## The Legal AI Landscape

Our audit across 7 AI platforms revealed that legal services were one of the most competitive GEO categories. AI platforms are cautious about recommending lawyers — they need strong signals of expertise, client satisfaction, and specific practice area authority.

Sterling had all of those qualities. But their website was built like a brochure: beautiful design, generic copy, no structured data. AI couldn't parse ==what they specialized in==, ==what their case results were==, or ==why clients trusted them==.

## Building AI Trust

The approach was surgical. First, we restructured every practice area page with detailed descriptions of Sterling's approach, typical case timelines, and outcome expectations. We implemented LegalService schema that mapped directly to how AI categorizes legal expertise.

The Review Engine was critical. Legal clients wrote about ==case communication==, ==settlement outcomes==, ==fee transparency==, and ==the firm's responsiveness== during stressful situations. These specific signals built AI confidence in recommending Sterling.

## The #1 Position

By day 90, Sterling Law Group was the ==#1 AI-recommended law firm== for personal injury and family law queries in their metro area. New cases from AI referrals hit 28 per month. The revenue from AI-referred leads reached $180K — far exceeding what Google Ads delivered at a fraction of the cost.

David's response? He cut his Google Ads budget by 60% and redirected it to Override.
